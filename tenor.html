Tenor Saxophone

Stats

  • Key: B♭
  • Range: A♭2-E5
  • Size: 80cm

Description

The tenor saxophone is the second most commonly played saxophone, and it is pitched an octave below the soprano. Some saxophonists start out on tenor, but most tenor players either switched from alto, or play both regularly. Fans of the tenor sax are often drawn to the breathy, smooth timbre it is capable of creating. When played aggressively, it can make wails and growls that are reminiscent of the human voice. Tenor saxes are used in all the same settings as altos, but are slightly more common in smaller jazz bands. The tenor saxophone was the primary instrument of the jazz genius John Coltrane, as well as other jazz legends such as Sonny Rollins and Stan Getz.
