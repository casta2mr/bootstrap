Baritone Saxophone

Stats

  • Key: E♭
  • Range: C2-A4
  • Size: 100cm

Description

The baritone saxophone (often shortened to 'bari sax') is the rarest of the four main saxophones, and is pitched an octave below the alto. When compared to other similar-ranged instruments such as euphoniums and cellos, the bari sax's timbre can be described as dirtier or edgier. Bari saxes are most commonly used in wind ensembles, sax quartets, and jazz big bands. Today, the bari sax is gaining exposure through Leo Pellegrino, a member of the band Too Many Zooz, and former member of Lucky Chops. He is most known for his unique percussive style of playing.
