Extreme High-Range Saxophones


Sopranissimo Saxophone

Stats

  • Key: B♭
  • Range: A♭4-D♭7
  • Size: 30cm

Description

The sopranissimo saxophone, or soprillo, is the smallest and highest pitched member of the saxophone family, pitched an octave above the soprano. It was invented in 2002 by the German company Benedikt Eppelsheim, which is the only company that currently makes them. The most well known soprillo player is Nigel Wood.



Sopranino Saxophone

Stats

  • Key: E♭
  • Range: D♭4-A6
  • Size: 50cm

Description

The sopranino saxophone is nowhere near as rare as the soprillo, but nowhere near as common as a soprano. It is pitched an octave above the alto sax, and is made by several high-end saxophone manufacturers.
