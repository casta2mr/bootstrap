Soprano Saxophone

Stats

  • Key: B♭
  • Range: A♭3-E6
  • Size: 70cm

Description

The soprano saxophone is the smallest and highest pitched of the four common saxophones, and the third smallest overall. Its timbre is similar to that of an oboe, but with a bit more "bite". It is occasionally used in classical and wind ensemble music, but its most prominent roles have been in saxophone quartets and jazz bands. Some notable soprano sax players are; Sidney Bechet, John Coltrane, and Kenny G.
